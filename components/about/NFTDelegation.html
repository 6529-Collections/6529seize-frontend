Reference Architecture

Wallets, Delegation, Consolidation

The purpose of this page is to describe what we consider a reference (good) architecture for organizing your ETH addresses and using the delegation and consolidation features of The Memes/6529 ecosystem, in a manner that will improve your address security.

Your personal circumstances may vary, no approach is fool-proof and you still have to get implement the below correctly, but the overall approach described should be a good approach for most people in the 6529 ecosystem.

Overview

It is important to take proper safety measures to keep your ETH, ERC-20 tokens and NFTs safe. Common ways to lose crypto assets are:
  • Phishing: Giving your seed phrase or access to your computer (through, say, screensharing) to anyone. The most effective mitigation for this is: "simply don't do it"
  • Malware: Malicious software on your computer can access the private key of a hot wallet like Metamask. The most effective mitigation for this is: “use a hardware wallet”
  • Connecting to malicious websites: Malicious websites can take your NFTs if you sign a malicious transaction, even if you use a hardware wallet.
The most effective mitigation for this is to use separate addresses for your “vault”, for your “transaction wallet” and for your “minting wallet”

There are other more obscure risks, but if you can organize your ETH activities to avoid these three risks, you are doing better than almost everyone in the ecosystem and we salute you!

Seize.io and NFTDelegation.com

The 6529 team has built and launched NFTDelegation, an open-source, trust-less smart contract for advanced delegation and consolidation. NFTDelegation.com is a smart contract that any team, project or individual can use.

It is being used first on Seize.io, but your entries are visible and available to other projects as well.

The combination of “delegation” (allowing one address to take actions on behalf of another one) and “consolidation” (requesting multiple addresses to be treated as one) means that a multi-address architecture like described below will still allow for convenient and safer NFT activities.

Reference Address Architecture

  1. Buy one hardware wallet ($60 to $200) from a reliable vendor like Trezor or Ledger. Consider using the "Shamir's Secret Sharing Scheme" (SSSS) to split your keys when setting it up.

  2. Create 3 Ethereum addresses on the hardware wallet

    1. Address A is your “vault” address. You send any NFTs that you plan to hold for long periods of time to this address. You do not connect these addresses to any services. You only transfer NFTs in or out from this address.

    2. Address B is your “transaction” address. You use this address to buy or sell NFTs on major, trusted marketplaces.

    3. Address C is your “minting” address. You use this address to mint NFTs and to connect to new, unknown and untrusted services. You do not keep any NFTs or any meaningful assets on this address. Minted NFTs should be transferred to Address A or Address B as appropriate, as soon as they are minted.

  3. Acceptable alternatives to the above:

    1. Address A (vault): Could be a SAFE (formerly Gnosis) multi-sig smart contract for greater security or resilience

    2. Address C (minting): Could be a hot wallet or mobile wallet for greater convenience

  4. [Optional] Label these addresses as subdomains of your ENS (e.g. mint.yourname.eth) for better legibility and reduce the likelihood of a transfer made in error.

Set Delegations

Setting delegations will allow you to mint any allowlist spots from any wallet from your “hottest”/ “most convenient” wallet. The best way to set delegations in a 3-address architecture is below.

  1. Set the following three (3) delegations on Seize.io

  2. Connect to Seize.io with Address A (vault)

    1. Designate Address B (transaction) as the subdelegate of Address B

    2. This means that Address A never has to connect to any service again and further delegations can be made on its behalf by Address B

  3. Connect to Seize.io with Address B (transaction)

    1. Address B (transaction) delegates Case 2 (minting/allowlist) to Address C (minting)

    2. Address B (transaction) delegates Case 2 (minting/allowlist) on behalf of Address A (vault) to Address C (minting)

    3. This means that all allowlist spots for Address A, B and C will be assigned to Address C which can mint on behalf of all three

  4. [Optional] You can delegate Case 3 (airdrops) from Address B and Address C to Address A so that all airdrops go directly to your vault. Do this if you are not planning to sell your airdrops, otherwise consider delegating them to Address B instead.

  5. We recommend that people set their delegation for “All Collections” unless you have some unique circumstances. We will respect “All Collections” and collection-specific delegations. If you make both types of delegations, we will respect the collection-specific delegation instead of the All Collections delegation.

Set Consolidations

Consolidations have nothing to do with delegation from a functionality perspective. They are an on-chain message to Seize to treat two or three addresses as one for the purpose of calculating metrics related to the content of your wallet, such as total cards held, total unique cards held, TDH and, also, to allow for a common view of your NFTs in on-site galleries.

You can choose to set consolidation for “All Collections” or just for “The Memes” contract and we will respect either one. If you set consolidation entries on both, we will respect the one for “The Memes” contract.

  1. Connect to Seize.io as Address B

    1. Address B sets a consolidation with Address A

    2. Address B sets a consolidation with Address C

    3. Address B sets a consolidation on behalf of Address A with Address B

    4. Address B sets a consolidation on behalf of Address A with Address C

  2. Connect to Seize.io as Address C

    1. Address C sets a consolidation with Address A

    2. Address C sets a consolidation with Address B

  3. Notes:

    1. You can consolidate more than 3 addresses, but Seize.io will only count the last 3 delegations you made for consolidation purposes (to limit gaming)

    2. The purpose of consolidation is simply to allow people to use a 3-address architecture safely without being penalized in allowlists.{" "}

    3. If we believe you or others are gaming the system (e.g. buying consolidation from a 3rd party), we reserve the right to apply penalties to you, such as removing your addresses from allowlists, or to change the system altogether, in our completely arbitrary discretion. In other words, please don’t FAFO.

I don’t have a hardware wallet

You should buy a hardware wallet.

In the meantime, you can still replicate this 3-address architecture in your hot wallet. While this does not protect you from malicious software on your computer, it will still reduce your risk in the interim.

This is too hard!

No, it is not, it is very easy, you just need to invest a few hours to understand it, just like you have to invest time for other important activities like driving or learning how to use a bank account.

If you are going to hold $1,000 to $1,000,000 of NFTs (or more) on-chain, you should take the time to learn how to do this. It will dramatically reduce your risk of losing your valuable and important NFTs and other cryptoassets.
